# 03. Research methods and coupling criteria

## What this directory shows

This directory presents a public-safe methodology surface for studying AI-assisted knowledge work without treating polished output as evidence by itself. It preserves the distinction among subject report, observed tool behavior, agent interpretation, and later human verification.

## Artifacts

- `artifacts/session-logging-protocol.md`: a generic dual-role protocol for sessions where the AI system is both studied phenomenon and data-collection instrument.
- `public-safe-thesis-scope.md`: a short public-safe summary derived from the thesis-refocus material.
- `selected-reflection-excerpts.md`: selected technical excerpts from July 2026 session records, retained with their individual and unverified status.

## Role in the wider architecture

The method layer gives the platform a way to learn from work without silently converting inference into fact. Coupling criteria, failure capture, and review checkpoints connect the retrieval proof to a human-centered research and design loop.

## Boundary

The research material concerns an N=1, self-directed study and generative design hypotheses. It does not establish clinical effects, population claims, or product performance. Raw session logs are intentionally excluded because they may contain private self-report.
