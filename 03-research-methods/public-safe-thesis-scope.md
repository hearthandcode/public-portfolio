# Public-safe thesis scope

## Status

This is a public-safe overview derived from a July 2026 thesis-refocus proposal. It is a proposal under review, not a completed thesis or a claim of institutional enrollment.

## Research direction

The work examines AI as a possible cognitive scaffold through the established Extended Mind literature, qualitative method, and applied AI systems design. Its central question is not whether AI produces a general productivity gain. It is how a persistent, reviewable human-AI working environment might support attention, memory, task initiation, authorship, and return to work for one researcher.

## Method boundary

The study is an N=1 autoethnographic research program. Its outputs are individual observations, methodological records, and generative design hypotheses. They are not clinical findings, population claims, or product-performance results.

## Platform relationship

Exocore is an applied artifact downstream of the research questions. Its proposed architecture supplies a place to test provenance, human authority, failure capture, and session continuity. The platform is not represented as completed or as proof that the research hypotheses are true.
